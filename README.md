# OpenSourceImplementationLSM_MicropolarTO

## Abstract 

In recent years, topology optimization of structural components incorporating the influence of material microstructural length scales has become a key area of research, especially with the advancement of additive manufacturing and the growing focus on robust microstructure designs. Classical continuum mechanics do not incorporate any microstructural length scales and can not capture experimentally observed size effects in advanced materials with heterogeneous microstructures. To overcome this limitation, various nonlocal topology optimization methods have been developed to account for size effects arising due to the presence of heterogeneities.  To capture size effects in the optimized shapes, the micropolar topology optimization method introduces microrotations as additional degrees of freedom in addition to the displacement field variables. In this work, we propose an open-source finite element implementation of a level-set-based micropolar topology optimization method using  \texttt{Gridap} and \texttt{GridapTopOpt} in the Julia programming language. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach through a representative two-dimensional benchmark problem. The results reveal a significant influence of the bending length scale and micropolar parameters on the optimized shapes, especially when the specimen dimensions are comparable to the bending length scale.
